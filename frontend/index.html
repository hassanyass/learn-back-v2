How It Works

Five steps from upload to mastery feedback.

1. Choose Your Topic

Upload lecture slides or pick a subject. The system segments your material into teachable concepts.

2. Teach KIDO

Explain concepts to KIDO in your own words. Teaching forces you to organize and articulate knowledge.

3. Answer Questions

KIDO asks follow-up questions to probe depth. The AI evaluator checks your reasoning in real time.

4. Interact with Widgets

Knowledge checkpoints appear as interactive quizzes and selection tasks to verify understanding.

5. Get AI Feedback

Receive a detailed mastery report with per-topic scores, misconception analysis, and improvement suggestions.

See It In Action

A simulated teaching session with KIDO.

LearnBack Session — Greedy Algorithms

You (Teacher)

A greedy algorithm makes the locally optimal choice at each step, hoping to find the global optimum. For example, in activity selection, we always pick the activity that finishes earliest.

KIDO

That makes sense! But why does picking the earliest finishing activity actually give us the most activities? Could you explain the greedy-choice property?

You (Teacher)

The greedy-choice property means that a locally optimal choice leads to a globally optimal solution. By choosing the earliest finish time, we leave the maximum remaining time for other activities.

KIDO — Knowledge Check

Which property guarantees that the greedy approach works for activity selection?

KIDO

Correct! One thing I want to clarify though — you mentioned greedy always finds the global optimum. That is only true when the greedy-choice property holds. Not all problems have this property. Can you think of a case where greedy fails?

KIDO — Session Feedback

Strong understanding of greedy fundamentals. Your explanation of the greedy-choice property was accurate. Area to revisit: distinguish when greedy applies vs. when dynamic programming is needed. Overall mastery: 78%

About the Project

LearnBack is an AI learning-by-teaching system where students teach a virtual AI student named KIDO. The system evaluates explanations in real time and provides structured feedback.

LLM Integration

Dual-agent architecture with evaluator and student agents powered by large language models.

RAG-Based Reasoning

Retrieval-augmented generation grounds KIDO's responses in your actual lecture content.

Misconception Detection

Identifies and addresses conceptual errors during teaching through targeted follow-up questions.

Adaptive Questioning

Bayesian Knowledge Tracing adapts difficulty based on demonstrated mastery level.

Why Learning by Teaching Works

Active Recall

Teaching forces you to retrieve and restructure knowledge from memory, one of the most effective study techniques proven by research.

Protege Effect

Students who teach others demonstrate deeper understanding and longer retention than those who only study for themselves.

Feedback Loops

Immediate, structured feedback after each explanation accelerates correction of misconceptions and strengthens retention.

What Makes LearnBack Different

Real-Time Misconception Detection

The evaluator agent identifies factual errors and conceptual gaps as you teach, not after. Corrections happen in the moment.

Adaptive Questioning Engine

KIDO adjusts question difficulty using Bayesian Knowledge Tracing. Weak areas get more probing; strong areas advance faster.

AI-Driven Feedback Loop

Every session produces a structured mastery report with per-topic scores, identified misconceptions, and actionable improvement paths.
